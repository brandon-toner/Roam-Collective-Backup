            - Agile projects are structured around...
                - Stories â€” bite size pieces of work
                - Sprints â€” collections of stories (work), time-bound
                - Epics â€” collections of sprints, oriented around a goal
            - Adapting the framework to our goals at Roam Collective, I propose the following structure...
                - Stories â†’ `#Q` blocks that develop into `[[Q:]]` pages (or other tasks)
                - Sprints â†’ a portion (or all of) an outline or collection of `Questions` to answer during a given time period
                - Epic â†’ an entire outline or collection of `Questions` to answer during an overarching `Question`

                        - "#Q What are the fundamental dependent relationships within a knowledge graph?"
                    - **With status:** `#Sprint/Current`

                    - **Nested under:** "`Sprint/1`"
                - `Sprint/1`

                    - **With status:** `#Sprint/Active`

            - Outlining an Epic would proceed like...
                1. Determine broad question to answer during epic
                    - Source of question can be:
